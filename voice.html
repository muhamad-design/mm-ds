Voice & content

Interface copy is part of the design: precise, verb-first, and free of filler.

Principles#

Copy is part of the design. Keep it precise, verb-first, and free of filler - the interface should read the way it works.

Rules#

Writing interface copy

  • Case. Title Case for buttons, labels, titles, and tabs; sentence case for body, helper text, and toasts.
  • Actions. Pair a verb with an object - Deploy Project, Delete Member - never a bare OK or Confirm.
  • Errors. Say what happened, then what to do next.
  • Toasts. Name the thing that changed, drop the trailing period, and never write successfully.
  • Empty states. Point at the first action the user should take.
  • Progress. Present participle plus an ellipsis: Deploying…, Saving….
  • Mechanics. Numerals for counts, curly quotes, a real ellipsis character; skip please and marketing superlatives.
